Midterm

Midterm project presentation

Present a proposed AWS-based infrastructure design for a small organization using foundational systems administration, cloud, identity, networking, compute, Linux, storage, and database concepts.

Scope

Presentation brief

A mid-course design presentation that demonstrates conceptual understanding and early cloud administration planning.

Recommended coverage

  • Systems administration foundations
  • Cloud and virtualization model choice
  • IAM and governance approach
  • VPC and network design
  • Compute, Linux, storage, and database choices

Expected output

  • One presentation deck
  • One architecture diagram set
  • One short design brief or speaking outline

CLO alignment

  • CLO 1
  • CLO 2
  • CLO 3

Delivery

Presentation structure

A concise technical presentation that focuses on design reasoning more than implementation detail.

Suggested flow

  1. Introduce case: Describe the organization, problem, and infrastructure needs.
  2. Explain design: Present the cloud architecture, core services, and administrative controls.
  3. Justify choices: Explain why the chosen models, services, and Linux administration approach are appropriate.
  4. Respond to questions: Defend the proposed design and operational assumptions.

Teacher evaluation focus

  • Conceptual correctness
  • Alignment between requirements and chosen AWS services
  • Clarity of diagrams and explanation
  • Professional communication

Final

Final project presentation

Present a complete cloud systems administration solution that includes deployment, Linux administration, monitoring, security, resilience, recovery, maintenance, and optimization decisions.

Scope

Capstone brief

A final presentation showing end-to-end systems administration judgment across the AWS-oriented course coverage.

Recommended coverage

  • Application or service architecture
  • Linux systems administration plan
  • Monitoring, logging, and security controls
  • Reliability, backup, and disaster recovery approach
  • Maintenance, optimization, and Well-Architected review

Expected output

  • One final presentation deck
  • One integrated architecture and operations diagram set
  • One final technical summary brief

CLO alignment

  • CLO 1
  • CLO 2
  • CLO 3
  • CLO 4

Delivery

Presentation structure

A capstone presentation that emphasizes integration, professional judgment, and defensible administration decisions.

Suggested flow

  1. Frame the solution: Describe the case, business needs, and technical goals.
  2. Present the environment: Explain the architecture, AWS services, Linux administration approach, and security model.
  3. Show operations planning: Present monitoring, backup, recovery, maintenance, and optimization decisions.
  4. Defend the design: Answer questions on trade-offs, risks, and administrative sustainability.

Teacher evaluation focus

  • Completeness of the end-to-end solution
  • Technical justification and trade-off awareness
  • Operational realism
  • Quality of presentation and response to questions
